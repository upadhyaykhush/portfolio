  • Support Vector Machine

    The idea of finding a hyperplane that best separates the features into different domains.

    In 2 Dimensions plane, the decision surface is a line, in 3 Dimensions it is a plane and beyond it is known as the Hyperplane.

    In this particular project we will have a hyperplane with 13 features. It is beyond imagination of having a 13-featured hyperplane, but the system does quite a useful job for us. Sometimes, the hyperplane is also called as Decision Surface. This hyperplane decides whether the data point lies under which classification as it is the decision surface.

    The decision surface is decided in such a way that it also leaves an equal space from each side of the hyperplane. This means that this decision surface will have a two layered coating over the actual decision surface and the distance between the decision surface and the two layers will be the same. This is called Margin.

    The layers are the justified by the closest two data points of a class which and after the layers are justified, we are able to get the margin which will be equidistant from both the layers and will be a decision surface for those two classes.


  • Hyper Parameters and Tuning

    Probability: The function to this parameter is whether to enable probability estimates. This must be enabled prior to calling fit, will slow down that method as it internally uses 5-fold cross-validation, and predict_proba may be inconsistent with predict.

    Kernel: The main function of the kernel is to transform the given dataset input data into the required form. There are various types of functions such as linear, polynomial, and radial basis function (RBF). Polynomial and RBF are useful for non-linear hyperplane. Polynomial and RBF kernels compute the separation line in the higher dimension. In some of the applications, it is suggested to use a more complex kernel to separate the classes that are curved or nonlinear. This transformation can lead to more accurate classifiers.
